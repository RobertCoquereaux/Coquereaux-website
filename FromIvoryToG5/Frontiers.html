Connecting the other way (working, not working...)
After having successfuly sent a screen from the Ivory to your G5, you could ask about doing the opposite, or, more modestly to do a remote login from the G5 Terminal (or from a X11 xterm) to the Ivory.
Telnet works fu-ine :  you have first to give access by typing (neti:remote-login-on) at the Lisp listener and then type “telnet the-Ivory-IP-number” in your OSX Terminal or in X11. One serious drawback is the keyboard. Keys don’t match and you don’t know what you are typing...
I did not succed with FTP (I mean typing FTP Ivory-IP-number from the G5) : the connection is established but I could neither list nor retrieve files from the Ivory in this way.  Maybe I did not try hard enough...  To retrieve file from the LMFS, I used th following technique: from the listener, copy some Ivory file to its host (the FX), then connect the G5 to the FX and mount the appropriate disk on the G5 desktop. Admitedly this is not a very smart way of exchanging files.
  
 
Other things to do in some future, or never (not working, working...)
- X11 :  One drawback is the keyboard. The correspondence between the Ivory and the G5 keyboard under X11 (I am not talking about the correspondence between the Ivory and the FX host) looks like what it would have been after having performed some  X11 connection with a Sun, with a keyboard of  type 4.  It is a bit akward...  For instance :  Select is F1, Function is F3, Susppend is F4, Resume is F5, Abort is F6, Super is F7, Hyper is F8. Symbol, Refresh, End, and one Help key  should be respectively F9, F10, F11  and F12, but they don’t wlork because F keys 9 to 12 are reserved by Apple.  Help is Help (!), Rubout is Suppress (the small key above the arrows), Scroll is the double-down arrow located above the usual arrow keys, Meta Scroll is the corresponding double - up arrow, Meta is Command,  M-click and R - click are respectively Alt-click and Command-click.  The nw-se arrow is another End key. The crossed box (above the 7 of the numeric keypad) calls the Input History. The four directional arrows work as they are supposed to.
- Main console : One day the presumably very old monitor attached to the Ivory host, and therefore playing the role of the main Ivory console will collapse (mine is a 20 years old 15” Apple monitor). This is clearly unacceptable. Connecting directly a new display to the Ivory and its host rises cable and plug issues, that can be solved (I managed to do it).  Then,  for the old FX to use the new display I had also to  replace the Apple Toby frame buffer by a Radius card, inserted on the Nubus, and I was able to display the old system 7 desktop ruuning the FX on a new monitor (an IIyama). Good. Unfortunately, the Ivory was very unhappy juste after booting Lisp (apparently it was not expecting such a display). I did not know how to fix this so I came back to my old monitor.
- It could be that the FX board will collapse, some day. Actually,I was worried about this,  twice. The first time it was just a battery problem (there is a special kind of battery plugged into the FX board, below the internal hard disk, sometimes it has to be changed : you know that it is time to do it when your machine - I mean the FX -  refuses to start from the leyboard). The second time it seemed more serious : I could start, but there was a shut down after 5 minutes. I was afraid that some component of the FX power supply had melted, but no, it was just the fan (and there is a securit y that prevents overheating).  The original fan, besides being noisy, is not very robust. I got a new fan, that I switch on manually (it is no longer driven by the keyboard, and it is not connected to the power supply,  like the previous one). In any  case, the noise of the new fan, plus the noise of the old SCSI disks is still a bit too much for our modern standards.  I can certainly get another, more silent, fan, but what to do with the SCSI disks ? Apparently the Ivory only wants to boot from FEP files written on disks located somewhere along the SCSI chain.  How to trade an SCSI disk by a new (and silent) disk with USB of Firewire connections ? No idea. I explained before how to make a backup of FEP files on such USB or Firewire disks, by using the network,  but  I don’t see how to attach them to the Lisp Machine.
- And now a totally mad suggestion : what about forgetting about the old host and just emulate it on a new machine, like a G5, by using the Basilisk emulator : it indeed emulates 68k machines and works fine (you need to use the ROM of a II or a quadra, but this should not a problem). But how will you attach the Ivory board to it since it has to be inserted in a Nubus? Well... an idea could be to use a Digidesign Nubus Expansion Chassis (I never saw one). Besides the fact that it may be quite difficult to find, I do not see how the emulator will ever notice the existence of such a Nubus expansion and I don’t even know how one could physically connect such an expansion chassis to a new machine ! OK, this last section was not very serious...  
It could also be that the Ivory board itself collapses one day.
